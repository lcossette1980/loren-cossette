The Interview Protection Checklist
Defending Against Interview-as-Extraction — Chapter 3 / Script 11

Multiple interview rounds can become organizational theft disguised as process. This checklist helps you recognize when an interview crosses from evaluation into free consulting, and provides protection protocols at each stage.

Pre-Interview Assessment
Research the organization’s AI maturity and current capabilities
Determine if the role is genuinely funded (ask directly)
Set a personal limit on rounds/hours of free demonstration
Round 1–2: Standard Evaluation
Present your background and approach (general, not proprietary)
Show results and outcomes, not full methodology
Share dashboards or outputs, not architecture
Answer technical questions at the conceptual level
Round 3+: Protection Protocols
Scope assessment: Is this still an interview or has it become consulting?
If presenting to multiple teams, limit scope per session
Present results without full methodology
Use the script: “We’re moving into territory closer to consulting. Let’s align on next steps.”
Document what you’ve shared (protect your IP after the fact)
Red Flags
“Can you build a quick prototype/demo for us?” (after round 2)
“We want you to present to the full engineering team” (extraction at scale)
Asking for specific technical solutions to their actual problems
No discussion of compensation, start date, or offer timeline by round 3
“We’ve decided to do it internally” (after extensive demonstrations)
Remember Your systems, your architecture, your solutions are proprietary intellectual property—whether or not you have formal IP protection. Protect them the way you’d protect any proprietary system, because that’s what they are.
