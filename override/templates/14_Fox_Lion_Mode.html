Machiavelli’s fox recognizes traps. His lion frightens wolves. Every engagement requires you to choose which mode serves the situation. This selector helps you make that choice deliberately rather than reactively.

Situation Assessment
Describe the Situation
 
Fox Mode

Recognizing political traps. Strategic understatement. Maneuvering around obstacles rather than through them.

Use Fox Mode when: The opponent has more positional power than you
Direct confrontation would cost you more than the win is worth
You need more information before committing to a position
The political landscape is unclear or shifting
You’re being set up (ambush meeting, gotcha question, smiling underminer)
Lion Mode

Standing with data and saying: “This objection has no supporting evidence. Here is mine.” Composure as authority.

Use Lion Mode when: You have documented evidence and the terrain is favorable
Your credibility is being publicly challenged and silence would validate the challenge
The audience needs to see strength and confidence from you right now
You’ve done the Contrast Protocol prep and you’re ready
A factual correction is needed and only you can provide it
