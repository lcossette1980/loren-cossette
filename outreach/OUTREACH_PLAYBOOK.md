# Outreach Playbook — Public-Sector AI Modernization

Templates for cold and warm outreach to state, county, and city government decision-makers. Pulled directly from what's working in the embedded role at Multnomah County.

---

## Targeting Principles

**Decision-makers in priority order:**

1. **Innovation / Digital Services Director** — usually owns AI budget, fastest yes
2. **CIO / Deputy CIO** — bigger budget, slower cycle, more procurement gating
3. **Accessibility Lead / ADA Coordinator** — best for compliance-driven entry (ADA Title II)
4. **Department Director with a stuck problem** — UCR-style legacy modernization openings often start here
5. **Chief Data Officer / CDO** — RAG / knowledge system openings

**Volume guidance:** 20-30 targeted outreaches per week beats 200 spray-and-pray. Personalize the opening line on every send. If you can't personalize, don't send.

**The single rule:** lead with proof, not pitch. The Multnomah County program does 80% of the work. Your job is to make the reader curious enough to click `multco-presentations.web.app`.

## CADENCE GUIDELINES

- **Initial outreach:** Mon-Wed 9-11am or Tue-Thu 1-3pm in target's time zone. Avoid Mondays before 9am, Fridays after 2pm, holidays.
- **Follow-up #1:** 5-7 calendar days after initial
- **Follow-up #2:** 10-14 days after follow-up #1, final touch
- **Then stop.** Re-engage in 3-6 months only if something changed (new initiative, new role, public RFP)
- **Total touches per contact:** 3 emails over ~4 weeks + 1-2 LinkedIn messages spread across the same period

If they reply with "not now" — believe them. Add to a 90-day re-touch list and move on.

## TRACKING

Minimum fields to track per outreach:
- Date sent
- Agency
- Contact name + role
- Channel (email, LinkedIn, referral)
- Custom hook used (what specific thing about them)
- Response (none / interested / not now / referred elsewhere)
- Next action + date

A Notion table or Airtable does this fine. Don't build a CRM until you have 200+ contacts in pipeline.

## SOURCEWELL / OMNIA / TIPS / NASPO

If you're going the cooperative-purchasing route, your prep list before the agency conversation:

1. Identify which cooperative the target agency uses (often listed on their procurement page)
2. Pull the list of IT / consulting master contract holders on that cooperative
3. Identify 2-3 holders whose master contracts allow AI specialist subcontracting
4. Reach out to those holders BEFORE pitching the agency — get a soft "yes we'd add you" in writing
5. When the agency asks "what vehicle?", name the cooperative + the prime contractor

If you skip step 4, you'll get the agency excited then stall on procurement structure — exactly the failure mode they're trying to avoid.
